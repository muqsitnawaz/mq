## How mq works
- `.tree("full")` shows file/section structure with content previews
- `.section("Name") | .text` extracts specific section content
- You can run multiple mq calls in parallel if needed

## Key insight: scope your queries
Running `.tree("full")` on a huge directory is expensive (thousands of chars just for structure).
Running it on a single file or small subdirectory is cheap and gives you exactly what you need.

Use your judgment:
- Small repo or obvious target? Tree it directly.
- Large repo or unclear? Explore briefly to find the right subdirectory first.
- Don't over-explore - a quick look at directory structure is enough to scope.

## Examples
```bash
# If you know the target file/dir
{{MQ_BIN}} {{REPO_PATH}}/docs/auth.md '.tree("full")'

# Extract specific section
{{MQ_BIN}} <file> '.section("OAuth") | .text'
```
